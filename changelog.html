Version 1.3

Activity & history

  • New "Activity" tab: a history of the changes made to your lists — additions, imports, deletions and applied ranges.
  • Undo and redo actions: from the Activity tab you can undo an operation or restore it, with a confirmation showing the affected numbers.
  • Appearance customization: a new "Appearance" tool to choose the colors and size of the history icons, with a customizable palette.
  • History statistics: a summary of activity broken down by type of change.

iCloud sync

  • Redesigned iCloud sync: lists and history sync across your devices and, on activation, the data is merged automatically.
  • Clearer sync: no more device/iCloud choice prompt when one of the two sides is empty — the data is aligned automatically.

Import & export

  • Reworked import and export flow: the import and export overlays have been redesigned for a clearer, more consistent and smoother experience.
  • Added a progress bar during import, showing the progress of the operation.
  • Cancelable import: you can stop an import in progress, with a more precise and reliable progress percentage.
  • Fixed an issue where, in the Import numbers from images feature, the related button could be hidden at large text sizes (Dynamic Type).
  • Fixed an issue where numbers pasted from messages could contain invisible characters that prevented them from being searched and imported.

Analysis

  • New layout for range rows: a chip with the index, the interval highlighted and a visual connector from the first number to the last.
  • More accurate range analysis: fixed some cases where overlapping ranges could be counted twice, and improved handling when the database limit is reached.
  • Faster analysis on large databases.
  • If the database is not readable, the app now reports it with a clear message instead of showing a generic empty list.

Lists & blocking

  • The list updates automatically when you return to the app after changes made by other extensions (e.g. sharing).
  • Full-screen confirmation after adding a number: an animated checkmark immediately confirms that the operation succeeded.
  • Added a button to retry the iOS blocking update, shown when the last operation did not succeed.

Settings & Premium

  • Refreshed Premium in Settings: a new intro card (hidden if you already have Premium) that also highlights unlocking beyond 500 blocked numbers, and a redesigned purchase confirmation screen.
  • Premium unlocks filters and undo: with Premium, the Lists and Activity filters and action undo are enabled automatically, with no dedicated option to turn on.
  • In Settings, the breakdown of the space used by the database now distinguishes Numbers, History and Other, in a scrollable box.

Interface & accessibility

  • Removed the "Extension active" notice that appeared at launch.
  • Several boxes now scroll correctly at large text sizes (Dynamic Type).
  • The "What's new" row in Settings shows the version currently in use.

Stability & performance

  • Fixed a rare crash that could occur at app launch when the device was locked.
  • Greater stability and reliability: a thorough review of database handling and the iOS blocking mechanism for solid performance even with very large lists, thanks to three centralized policies: one for writes, one for reloading the iOS block (reload) and one for the database schema (SQLite).

Version 1.2

  • UI improvements and the fix of an annoying bug that could cause the app to crash in the background.
  • Refactored the CallKit reload with a new technique, which made it possible to raise the database limit to 500,000 numbers — double the previous version. Measurements show no issues even on older devices.
  • Free-version limit raised to 500 numbers active in iOS blocking.
  • Completely reworked the logic for discarded ranges. It moved from a fixed-threshold criterion (ranges over 500 numbers with fewer than 15 origins) to a density-based one: a range is discarded when its Origin numbers don't reach at least 0.65% of the covered numbers (examples in the online guide).
  • Added the option to import contacts from your address book. The range of import methods is now truly complete.
  • Added number recovery from screenshots (OCR). Re-importing the iOS blocked list is now quick and easy, with a step-by-step guide explaining how.
  • Added a comprehensive online guide for clarifications on every feature of the app.
  • New "Links" section in Settings gathering the user guide, what's new, privacy policy, and support contact.
  • Reworked the Analyze section to make the explanation of the search parameters clearly visible, now unified with the ranges explanation; the info has its own dedicated button.
  • Reworked the Allowed list. Since it has no range logic, the filters and the legend in its popup — previously identical to the Blocked one — were adjusted.

  • Limit der kostenlosen Version auf 500 Nummern erhöht, die in der iOS-Sperre aktiv sind.
  • Logik der verworfenen Bereiche vollständig überarbeitet. Vom festen Schwellenwert-Kriterium (Bereiche über 500 Nummern mit weniger als 15 Quellen) wurde auf ein Dichte-Kriterium umgestellt: Ein Bereich wird verworfen, wenn die Quell-Nummern nicht mindestens 0,65 % der abgedeckten Nummern erreichen (Beispiele in der Online-Anleitung).
  • Option zum Importieren von Kontakten aus dem Adressbuch hinzugefügt. Die Auswahl an Importmethoden ist nun wirklich vollständig.
  • Wiederherstellung von Nummern aus Screenshots (OCR) hinzugefügt. Die iOS-Sperrliste erneut zu importieren ist jetzt einfach und schnell, mit einer Schritt-für-Schritt-Anleitung.
  • Umfassende Online-Anleitung hinzugefügt für Fragen zu allen Funktionen der App.
  • Neuer Abschnitt „Links“ in den Einstellungen, der Anleitung, Neuigkeiten, Datenschutzerklärung und Support-Kontakt bündelt.
  • Bereich Analyse überarbeitet, damit die Erklärung der Suchparameter gut sichtbar ist, jetzt mit der Erklärung der Bereiche vereint; die Informationen haben eine eigene Schaltfläche.
  • Liste der Erlaubten überarbeitet. Da sie keine Bereichslogik hat, wurden die Filter und die Legende ihres Pop-ups – zuvor identisch mit der der Blockierten – angepasst.
